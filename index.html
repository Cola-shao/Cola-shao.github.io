What is This Challenge?

The primary purpose of this Challenge is to address critical bottlenecks in prenatal Congenital Heart Disease (CHD) screening via fetal echocardiography (FE). These bottlenecks include heavy reliance on highly specialized clinicians, inefficient manual annotation, diagnostic variability, data scarcity, and domain shiftâ€”all of which hinder AI-driven automation. The Challenge focuses on two core tasks: fetal cardiac ultrasound view segmentation and biometric measurement. Its goal is to drive the development of robust, clinically translatable AI algorithms. To achieve this, it prioritizes evaluating three key algorithm capabilities: segmentation/measurement accuracy, few-shot learning adaptability, and cross-clinical-setting generalization.

Tasks

To advance automated evaluation of carotid plaques via ultrasound, it is crucial to develop highly robust methods for two core tasks:

Track 1: Fetal Heart Segmentation:
Dice Similarity Coefficient (DSC): Is used to measure the region error.
95th percentile Hausdorff Distance (HD95): Is used to measure the boundary error.
Running time: Is used to measure the inference speed.

Track 2: Fetal Heart Biometric Measurement:
Mean Absolute Error (MAE): Is used to measure the average measurement error.
Intraclass Correlation Coefficient (ICC): Is used to measure measurement reliability and agreement.
Running time: Is used to measure the inference speed.

Sponsors

Mindray Bio-Medical Electronics Co., Ltd is the principal sponsor of the Challenge.
As a global leader in medical imaging, Mindray prioritizes advancing ultrasound for prenatal care. It has invested heavily in R&D to develop cutting-edge ultrasound systems. These systems include specialized transducers and AI-enhanced imaging, which support high-quality fetal cardiac imaging for millions of screenings each year. By sponsoring this Challenge, Mindray aims to achieve three key goals:
Foster innovation: Enable global researchers to refine top-tier algorithms for fetal cardiac AI.
Bridge clinical-technical gaps: Connect engineers and clinicians, ensuring solutions align with real-world needs.
Advance standardization: Support the development of benchmark datasets and metrics for consistent clinical practice.
Ultimately, these efforts will empower healthcare providers with precise, accessible fetal cardiac tools. This will help reduce missed CHD diagnoses and improve maternal-infant outcomes.
