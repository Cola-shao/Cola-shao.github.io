What is This Challenge?

Ischemic stroke is a highly disabling, recurrent, and economically burdensome disease, with approximately 10 million new cases worldwide each year. Carotid atherosclerotic plaque progression and rupture, leading to cerebral hypoperfusion and vascular occlusion, are major causes of ischemic stroke. Ultrasound due to its real- time imaging capability and absence of radiation has become the primary modality for carotid plaque assessment. Through accurate carotid plaque characterization, we aim to improve the efficiency of clinical decision-making related to stroke diseases, reduce the workload of medical personnel, and provide affordable diagnostic support for resource- limited areas, thereby promoting healthcare equity.

Challenges

To advance automated evaluation of carotid plaques via ultrasound, it is crucial to develop highly robust methods for two core tasks:

Track 1: Fetal Heart Segmentation:
Dice Similarity Coefficient (DSC): Is used to measure the region error.
95th percentile Hausdorff Distance (HD95): Is used to measure the boundary error.
Running time: Is used to measure the inference speed.

Track 2: Fetal Heart Biometric Measurement:
Mean Absolute Error (MAE): Is used to measure the average measurement error.
Intraclass Correlation Coefficient (ICC): Is used to measure measurement reliability and agreement.
Running time: Is used to measure the inference speed.
