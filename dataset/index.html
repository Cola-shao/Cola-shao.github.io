Dataset

Curate a Clinically Representative FE Dataset:​
Aggregate a high-quality, clinically representative FE image dataset from multiple clinical centers and diverse ultrasound devices (e.g., GE Voluson E10, Philips EPIQ 7), incorporating both labeled cases (for semi-supervised model training and internal validation) and unlabeled cases (to enable unsupervised or semi-supervised learning strategies). The dataset will be designed to capture real-world FE image characteristics (e.g., motion artifacts, speckle noise) and patient diversity (e.g., gestational age ranges), ensuring alignment with clinical practice.​

First large-scale public fetal echocardiography (FE) dataset: ​
​The dataset covers normal/abnormal fetal cardiac cases. Curated to capture real-world FE features (e.g., motion artifacts, speckle noise) and patient diversity (e.g., gestational age variations), it ensures clinical alignment and real-world relevance for algorithm assessments.
Multi-center: ​from multiple clinical centers​.
Multi-device: includes GE Voluson E10, Philips EPIQ 7 and other diverse ultrasound models.​
Multi-view & multi-parameter: 13 anatomical segmentations + 11 biometric parameters (aligned with clinical CHD diagnosis; validated by ISUOG).​
Semi-supervised: fits real-world clinical annotation Challenges (reduces exhaustive expert labeling).​
Rigorous evaluation: dual-validation (internal: stratified subset; external: unseen cohorts) + key metrics (DSC, HD95, MAE, ICC).​
Open sharing: post-Challenge access to de-identified dataset (HIPAA/GDPR-compliant) & open-source evaluation code.​
